Camel


Click to know how to make an origami camel:


Camel, (genus Camelus), either of three species of large ruminating hoofed mammals of arid Africa and Asia known for their ability to go for long periods without drinking. The Arabian camel, or dromedary (Camelus dromedarius), has one back hump, while the domesticated Bactrian camel (C. bactrianus) and the wild Bactrian camel (C. ferus) have two.

Here are some interesting facts about camels:

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  • Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.

Chameleon

A chameleon sits motionlessly on a tree branch. Suddenly its sticky, two-foot-long tongue snaps out at 13 miles an hour, wrapping around a cricket and whipping the yummy snack back into the reptile’s mouth.

Here are some interesting facts about chameleons:

  • Their feet work like salad tongs.
  • Almost half of all known chameleon species live in Madagascar.
  • Chameleons vary widely in terms of size.
  • Skin crystals enable chameleons to change their skin colour at will.
  • Unlike many lizards, chameleons can't regrow their tail.

Cicada


Click to know how to make an origami cicada:


Cicadas are members of the superfamily Cicadoidea and are physically distinguished by their stout bodies, broad heads, clear-membraned wings, and large compound eyes.

Here are some interesting facts about cicadas:

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  • Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

Panda



Click to know how to make an origami panda:



The giant panda has an insatiable appetite for bamboo. A typical animal eats half the day—a full 12 out of every 24 hours—and relieves itself dozens of times a day.

Here are some interesting facts about pandas:

  • A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Pigeon


Click to know how to make an origami pigeon:


Here are some interesting facts about pigeons:

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

Teddy Bear

Here are some interesting facts about bears:

  • Bears grieve deeply for others. Cubs are known to moan and cry when separated from their mothers.
  • Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees. They can use these for hiding, eating and even sleeping.
  • Bears were often honoured in the cultures of many early civilisations. They were seen as a symbol of power, strength and love.
  • The brown bear is Finland’s national animal.
  • They have far superior navigation skills to humans.
